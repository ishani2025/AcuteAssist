Chunk 2
Clinical Context:
A stroke occurs when blood flow to a part of the brain is interrupted, either due to a blocked artery (ischemic stroke) or a ruptured blood vessel (hemorrhagic stroke). In the context of the PDF, Acute Ischemic Stroke (AIS) is emphasized — this is the most common type, caused by obstruction of cerebral arteries, leading to brain tissue damage due to lack of oxygen.

Doctor Action Steps:
1. Ensure blood pressure is below 185/110 mmHg before rtPA.
2. Order urgent CT scan to differentiate ischemic vs hemorrhagic stroke.
3. Record exact symptom onset time.
4. Perform FAST neurological assessment.
5. Assess airway, breathing, and circulation.

Emergency Level: HIGH
